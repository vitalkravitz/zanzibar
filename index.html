About the island

Zanzibar is a Tanzanian archipelago off the coast of East Africa.
On its main island, Unguja, familiarly called Zanzibar, it's capital; Stone Town, a historic trade center with Swahili and Islamic influences.
Its winding lanes present minarets, carved doorways and 19th-century landmarks such as the House of Wonders, a former sultan’s palace.
The northern villages Nungwi and Kendwa have wide beaches lined with hotels.

The Resort

Utulivu Bungalow is a cozy yet convenient accommodation surrounded by a lush garden located in the Nungwi - Northern Coast of Zanzibar Island where you can discover Nungwi beach only after 15-minute walk. It’s perfect for young adventurous backpackers which makes it the best way to experience Zanzibar to the fullest. The bungalows has only 9 rooms with private veranda, 3 with Pool view and 6 with views of the hotel grounds and gardens. The rooms, all presented in timelessly elegant Zanzibar Island style, are very spacious, well appointed, and fully air-conditioned.

Geography

Zanzibar is one of the Indian Ocean islands. It is situated on the Swahili Coast, adjacent to Tanganyika (mainland Tanzania). The northern tip of Unguja island is located at 5.72 degrees south, 39.30 degrees east, with the southernmost point at 6.48 degrees south, 39.51 degrees east.

Population

The population of Zanzibar, a semi-autonomous region in Tanzania, was projected to reach some 1.7 million inhabitants as of 2021. According to projections, the number of people living in the archipelago might grow to nearly 1.9 million in 2025.

Stone Town

Stone Town is known for its ancient architecture and street food culture and is a major attraction for many tourists, who can relax in knowing that in addition to Swahili, English is widely spoken. The town is safe for tourists and solo travellers, and locals are only too eager to help.
